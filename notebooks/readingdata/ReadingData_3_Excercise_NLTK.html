Bonus: Zipf‚Äôs law¬∂

Zipf‚Äôs law is claimed to be a general law in language, that in any natural language text the frequency of every word is inverse to its rank in the frequency table. This means, that if you order all words of a text by their occurance, and give them a number related to their position in the ordered list, a log-log plot of (rank,frequency) should be linear.
